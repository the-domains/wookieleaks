The Alliance Constitution, for reference.

We the People of THE ALLIΔNCE, in Order to form a more perfect galactic coalition, so ordain and establish this Constitution for THE ALLIΔNCE.

Article 1

Section 1. All legislative powers herein granted shall be vested in a Senate of THE ALLIΔNCE.

Section 2. The Senate shall be comprised of two Senators from each THE ALLIΔNCE Guild with a minimum of forty members and each Senator shall have one Vote. If a Guild has a minimum of twenty-five members, and less than forty members, the Guild will have one Senator with one Vote only. Any Guilds with less than twenty-five members will not have a Vote in the Senate. The maximum number of Senators for a cluster is ten and can be selected from any guild in the cluster with a maximum of two per guild.

Section 3. Should both Senators from a Guild be unable to submit a Vote, a single Senator's Vote shall count for both both Senators in any Vote at the 48 hour mark after the Vote opens. Otherwise, if all Senators from a Guild are unable to submit their Vote no Vote shall be cast by that Guild.

Section 4. The selection of Senators is a Guild power and the method of selection shall remain the individual right of each Guild.

Section 5. No changes or amendments to this Constitution will occur without a two-thirds majority Vote of the entire Senate. In all other Votes, except Elections, a two-thirds majority of the Voting Senators is required to pass any new legislation.

Section 6. Two-thirds of the entire Senate has to Vote to pass a Vote. This constitutes a Quorum.

Section 7. All Voting will be done via online public roll call with 48 hours for the Senators to respond before the Vote is completed. If at any time the Vote achieves a two-thirds majority of the entire Senate in favor of the proposal the Vote is considered completed. If it takes longer than 48 hours to reach the required number of Senators Voting than the Vote will remain open. Additionally, if 48 hours has passed on a Vote and there is no two-thirds majority in favor of a proposal then the proposal will remain open. If at any time a two-thirds majority of the entire Senate Voting in favor of a proposal is not mathematically possible the Vote will be considered completed and the proposal will not be passed.

Section 8. Any Votes to Abstain will not be counted towards evaluating the total required to pass a Vote.

Section 9. Only a Senator can petition the Senate Spokesperson to hold a Vote. Another Senator, from a different guild, is required to second a petition to hold the Vote. The Senate Spokesperson is then required to bring the petition to the Senate for a Vote in a timely manner after 24 hours of discussion by the Senate. Both the original and seconded Senator cannot be the same person.

Section 10. To be eligible to Vote the Guilds must submit their Senator selections 24 hours prior to a Vote taking place to the Senate Spokesperson. The Senate Spokesperson is responsible for maintaining a publically viewable list of the current Senators for each Guild.

Section 11. You must actually be a member in the Guild to be a Senator for that Guild to Vote for a Guild.

Section 12. A once a month non-mandatory meeting will be scheduled by the Senate Spokesperson for the Senate to meet.

Article 2

Section 1. Guilds have all rights to manage themselves except where ceded in this Constitution.

Section 2. Guilds are strongly encouraged to add, or replace a character with, a Δ symbol to their guild name upon joining THE ALLIΔNCE.

Section 3. Each Member is strongly encouraged to add a Guild designation to their Discord names as determined by the member Guild.

Section 4. All members are expected to keep conversation on THE ALLIΔNCE main discord server clean and minor-safe outside of the adult-only sections maintained by the Discord Administrator.

Section 5. Removal of a Guild from THE ALLIΔNCE shall only occur with a two-thirds majority Vote of the Voting Senators. In such a proceeding the Senators representing the Guild proposed for removal shall have no Vote.

Section 6. All new Guilds added to THE ALLIΔNCE will orient during a two-week welcome period. During this time they will not be eligible for being added to the recruiting rotation and cannot Vote.

Section 7. A cluster is a sub-community of Alliance guilds who share resources; this could include but not limited to a Discord server, Members, and Leadership. It also has a system in place that moves players within its sub-community.

Article 3

Section 1. The Senate Spokesperson, Live Show and Online Coordinator, Head of Recruiting and Senate Examiner shall be elected by the Senate every 3 months on the last week of the month. To be considered for any of these positions you must be nominated, and seconded, by two different Senators from two different guilds who are not the same person. The terms for these positions will start on January 1st, April 1st, July 1st and October 1st of each calendar year.

Section 2. The Senate will elect a Webmaster and Discord Administrator that is nominated, and seconded, by two different Senators from two different guilds who are not the same person. At any time a Senator can call for a majority two-thirds Vote to remove either the Webmaster or Discord Administrator. Otherwise these roles continue until either the Webmaster or Discord Administrator retire from their position.

Section 3. For all Elections, for positions outlined in this Constitution, the Senate will Vote and the winner will be selected based upon the candidate that receives the most Votes for the position. Elections do not require a two-thirds majority Vote but do require the Quorum to be reached to elect a candidate.

Section 4. No single person can be elected to more than one elected position at any given time.

Section 5. To be eligible to be Voted into the positions of The Senate Spokesperson, Live Show and Online Coordinator, Webmaster, Discord Administrator, Head of Recruiting and the Senate Examiner you must be a member in good standing one of the already established THE ALLIΔNCE Guilds.

Section 6. Should there be a tie Vote in any Senate Vote, The Senate Spokesperson shall cast a Vote to break the tie and determine the outcome. The Senate Spokesperson does not have a Vote unless there is a tie.

Section 7. All Voting will be done publically via roll call. The Senate Spokesperson is responsible for assigning a member to be the Scribe to record the voting.

Section 8. Any Senator can request an examination of a dispute by the Senate Examiner. The Senate Examiner will then investigate the issue, write a report to be viewed by the entire Senate, and provide their recommendation to the requesting Senator. The requesting Senator will then decide what they want to put to Vote for resolving the matter to the Senate.

Section 9. The Webmaster, and any assistants they choose, will be empowered to remove any content posted to the website at their discretion.

Section 10. The Live Show/Online Video Coordinator, and any assistants they choose, will be empowered to remove any video content posted to the Website at their discretion.

Section 11. The Discord Administrator is responsible for selecting Discord Admins for the centralized THE ALLIΔNCE Discord server.

Section 12. The Discord Administrator, and their selected Discord Admins, are charged with the responsibility to enforce clean and respectful chat by all members on THE ALLIΔNCE main Discord server. They are empowered to ban members or delete offensive comments from THE ALLIΔNCE main Discord Server.

Section 13. Any member who is banned from THE ALLIΔNCE main Discord Server may petition the Senate for reinstatement. A majority two-thirds Vote will be cast by the Senate after both the member and the Discord Admin present why the banning occurred. The member must submit the request to the Senate Spokesperson for reinstatement. The Senate Spokesperson is then required to setup the Vote for reinstatement at the next available Voting opportunity.

Section 14. The help chosen by the Webmaster, Live Show and Online Coordinator and Discord Administrator must be approved by the Senate via a Vote prior to being assigned to the position.

Article 4

Section 1. The Head of Recruiting will set the minimums required for each tier of recruiting upon assuming office.

Section 2. Each tier will operate on a rotation basis. As members are needed for a Guild the Guild will update the tracking mechanism and will be placed at the bottom of the rotation. As members are placed in Guilds they will move to the bottom of the rotation.

Section 3. Each Guild may place themselves in a lower tier, compared to what they qualify for, should they so choose.

Section 4. The Head of Recruiting is empowered to make decisions regarding whether a situation warrants having additional recruits go to a Guild due to extenuating circumstances.

Section 5. Transfers to a lower Guild, defined by the Guild’s current ability to take down the raid content in the game, will never result in priority being given to a guild to replenish their membership.

Section 6. Transfers to the same level Guild, or a higher Guild, defined by the Guild’s current ability to take down the raid content in the game, shall result in that Guild being given priority in Recruiting for each member transferred until they are replenished. The player transferring must initiate the transfer in order for their former Guild to receive priority. The player transferring must initiate the transfer in order for their former Guild to receive priority.

Section 7. If a Guild receives a Transfer they will be placed at the bottom of their tier's rotation.

Section 8. If the current openings for Guilds is below 2% of the total available membership in all Guilds of a tier then a Vote of the Senate is required to add a new Guild to a tier to THE ALLIΔNCE. Otherwise a two-thirds majority Vote of the entire Senate is required to add a new Guild to a tier of THE ALLIΔNCE.
